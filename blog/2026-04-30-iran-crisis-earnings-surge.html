Iran Crisis Meets Earnings Surge: 3 Swing Trade Setups for May 2026

April 30, 2026 | SignalAlpha Daily Analysis

Two forces are colliding in markets right now. On one side: the Strait of Hormuz crisis has pushed Brent crude to $126 -- a four-year high -- with Goldman estimating exports through the chokepoint have fallen to 4% of normal levels. On the other: Q1 earnings season is delivering some of the strongest beats in years from Alphabet, Eli Lilly, and Caterpillar.

The result is a market that's moving fast but moving differently across sectors. Energy benefits. Healthcare accelerates. Tech splits between those spending wisely (Alphabet) and those burning cash with no clear ROI (Meta).

Here are three cause-and-effect setups we're tracking.

The Macro Picture

Iran-US Standoff: Trump rejected Tehran's proposal to reopen the Strait of Hormuz. Naval blockade remains. Goldman estimates Hormuz exports at 4% of normal -- that's 20% of global oil supply effectively shut. Brent touched $126.41 intraday before pulling back to $114. No resolution in sight.

Q1 GDP came in at 2.0%, below the 2.3% estimate. The Fed held rates steady but the vote was 8-4 -- the most divided since the early 1990s. Higher oil means higher inflation means the Fed is stuck. This backdrop favors companies with pricing power and commodity exposure over rate-sensitive growth names.
